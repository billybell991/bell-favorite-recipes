## Ingredients

- 2 cups mashed potatoes (preferably made with butter and cream)
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 large egg
- 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder (optional)
- Olive oil spray (or any cooking spray)

## Instructions

1. Prepare the Mashed Potatoes:If you haven’t already, prepare mashed potatoes. Ensure they are smooth and slightly on the thicker side (not too runny).
2. Mix the Ingredients:In a large bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, shredded cheddar cheese, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder (if using). Stir until the cheese is evenly distributed.
3. Form the Balls:Scoop about 1 tablespoon of the mashed potato mixture and roll it into a ball. Continue until all the mixture is used. You should be able to form 12–16 balls, depending on their size.
4. Coat the Balls:Set up a breading station:In one shallow dish, place the flour.In another dish, beat the egg.In a third dish, add the panko breadcrumbs.Roll each mashed potato ball first in the flour, then dip in the egg, and finally coat it in the panko breadcrumbs. Press gently to ensure the breadcrumbs stick well.
5. Preheat the Air Fryer:Preheat your air fryer to 375°F (190°C) for about 3-5 minutes.
6. Air Fry the Mashed Potato Balls:Lightly spray the air fryer basket with olive oil.Place the potato balls in the air fryer basket, leaving some space between them for air circulation.Lightly spray the tops of the balls with more olive oil.Air fry at 375°F for 8-10 minutes or until they are golden brown and crispy, flipping them halfway through the cooking time for even crispiness.
7. Serve:Once golden and crispy, remove the mashed potato balls from the air fryer. Let them cool slightly before serving.You can serve these with a dipping sauce, such as sour cream, ranch, or ketchup!

Tips:

- If the mashed potatoes are too soft and difficult to roll, refrigerate the mixture for about 30 minutes to firm it up.
- You can also add some cooked bacon bits or herbs (like chives or parsley) into the mixture for extra flavor.
