## Ingredients

- 6 to 8 baking apples, such as McIntosh
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- ½ cup coarsely chopped dried apricots or raisins (optional)
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 2/3 cup brown sugar
- ½ cup brown or granulated sugar
- ½ cup healthy margarine
- 3 Tbsp all-purpose flour
- 2/3 cup blanched slivered almonds (optional)

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Peel, core and slice apples. Place in a large bowl with apricots or raisins, if using. Stir ½ cup sugar with 3 Tbsp flour and cinnamon. Sprinkle over apples and toss to coat evenly.

2. Place in a 9-inch glass pie plate and pat down. Stir flour and brown sugar together. Using a pastry cutter or fork, cut margarine into flour/sugar mixture until crumbly. Mix in almonds, if using. Spread over apples.

3. Bake in center of preheated oven until apples are fork-tender, 45 to 50 minutes.

Serves 8 to 10.

1 Serving:- 322 Calories, 2 gr Protein, 10 gr Fat, 58 gr Carbohydrate

TIP: For a nice crumbly topping, freeze margarine for 10 minutes to make cutting in easier.
