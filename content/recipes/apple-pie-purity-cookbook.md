## Instructions

Preheat over to 375 degrees.

For each pie, I combine slightly more than ½ cup of sugar (more if apples are tart) with ½ tsp cinnamon and a dash of salt.

Prepare pastry and line a 9” pie plate with the rolled pastry..Roll out the top pastry.

Peel and core apples. (I usually used 6 medium-sized apples). I half fill the pastry-lined pie plate with thinly sliced apples. Sprinkle on half of the sugar mixture. Finish filling the pie plate with thinly sliced apples, heaping them in the center. Sprinkle the rest of the sugar mixture on top. Dot with 1 Tablespoon of butter.

Moisten edge of pastry. Place top crust in position. Seal edge and flute. Bake in preheated oven for 40 to 50 minutes, or until pastry is golden brown and the apples are tender.

NOTE: I usually freeze these pies before baking. To bake, I put the unbaked pie in a cold oven, then turn on the oven to 375 degrees and bake for 1 hour or until pie crust is golden brown.
