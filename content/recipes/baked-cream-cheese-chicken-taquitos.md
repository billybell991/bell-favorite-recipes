## Ingredients

- 8-10 flour or corn tortillas*
- 1 cup shredded chicken
- 2 oz. cream cheese softened (reduced fat will work)*
- 1/2 cup shredded Monterey-jack cheese
- 1 clove garlic minced (1/2 tsp.)
- 1/4 cup salsa verde  (I used normal red salsa)
- 1/4 tsp. ground cumin (I didn't use this)
- salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or foil (spray well if using foil) and set aside.
2. In a large bowl, mix together the chicken, cream cheese, Monterey-jack cheese, garlic, salsa verde, and ground cumin until well combined. Season with salt & pepper to taste.
3. Spoon two tablespoons of the chicken mixture into each tortilla. Roll each tortilla tightly and place seam side down on the baking sheet. You can secure the tortillas with toothpicks to hold them together a little better.
4. Bake for 15-17 minutes, broil for the last minute or two to get them a little more crispy.
5. Serve immediately.

### Recipe Notes

If using corn tortillas, make sure to microwave them for 45 seconds-1 minute. This will make them a little easier to work with and less likely to tear.

To soften cream cheese quickly, microwave for 15-30 seconds.

Freeze taquitos for up to two months in an air tight bag.
