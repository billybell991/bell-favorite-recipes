IT’S EASY TO MAKE BEAUTIFUL COOKIES – Brown Bag Cookie Art

1. Lightly oil & flour the mold.

2. Press the dough into the mold.

3. Knock the cookie out of the mold.

4. Bake cookies and enjoy.

GENERAL INSTRUCTIONS;

A) For best detail, mix your dough by hand.

B) Chill the dough well.

C) Now, using a pastry brush, lightly oil your mold with vegetable oil. Use a paper towel to wipe out as much oil as possible.

D) Dust the mold with flour, as you would a cake pan. Tap the edge of the mold on a cutting board to remove any excess flour. You will have to re-flour the mold before each cookie, but DO NOT re-oil it.

E) Take a piece of dough, and press sit firmly and evenly into the mold.

F) Using a sawing motion with a sharp knife, trim the back of the cookie so it is flush with the mold. To give your cookie a nice clean edge, use your thumb to pull the dough slightly away from the edges of the mold.

G) Now hold the mold perpendicular to a wooden cutting board and strike the edge sharply 3 or 4 times. Rotate the mold and strike it again a couple of times. The cookie should come right out.

H) Bake the formed cookie on a baking sheet in the top third of your oven until nicely browned – start checking at 10 minutes.

HELPFUL HINTS;

A) Mix by hand.

B) If cookies don’t come out of the mold easily, you may have used too much oil. The flour acts as the separator, not the oil. Clean the oily mold with a dry brush, re- flour, and try again.

C) Don’t be timid about rapping the mold quite hard on a wooden surface, If you hold the mold and strike it on its edge, it won’t break. The cookie will come right out.

D) If your finished cookies are puffing up and losing detail, use a smaller egg. If the cookies are spreading out and losing detail, use less sugar.

E) If you feel your dough is just too soft, add another 2 Tablespoons of flour.

F) Don’t substitute ingredients. You can experiment with other recipes and ingredients once you’ve mastered these.
