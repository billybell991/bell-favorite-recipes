- 1/3 Cup soy sauce
- 3 Tbs Brown Sugar
- 2 Tsp Worcestershire Sauce
- 1/4 Tsp Onion Powder
- 1/4 Tsp Ground Ginger
- 1/4 Tsp Garlic Powder
- 1/4 Tsp Liquid Smoke
- 1/2 Tsp Table Salt

- 1 Lb of Lean Beef, fat removed (about 1 inch thick)

Mix first 8 ingredients in bowl.  Set aside.

Cut meat across the grain into 1/2 to 1/4 inch (3 to 6 mm) thick slices.  Do not cut too thick or center won't dry.  Using partially frozen meat makes this easier to do.  Add beef strips to ingredients in bowl.  Stir to coat.  Cover.  Let stand in refrigerator overnight.  Draw strips across edge of bowl to remove excess marinade.  Place wire cooling tracks in baking pans with sides.  Arrange single layer of beef strips over and rearrange pans at half time.  Fills 2 tall, narrow jars.  Store airtight in cool location or in refrigerator.  Freeze for long-term storage.  Makes 1/2 pound (225g).

NOTES:

- I make this with a food dehydrator.  Really depends on thickness of the meat, but typically takes 6-8 hours in there.  I like it tough and chewy.
- This is a really tasty recipe.  I'd give it a 4.5 out of 5.
- I also like to throw in around 1 Tsp of crushed red pepper to spice it up a bit.
