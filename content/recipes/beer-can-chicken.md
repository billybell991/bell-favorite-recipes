- 1 can (12 oz.) beer
- 1 chicken (3 1/2 to 4 lbs)
- 2 tbsp or your favourite chicken rub (or Basic Barbecue Rub)

Pop the tab off the beer can.  Using a church key-style can opener, make a few more holes in the top of the can.  Pour out half the beer into your mouth and enjoy!

Set up the grill for indirect grilling (ie: hot side not where you're putting the bird) and preheat to medium.  If using a charcoal grill, place a large drip pan in the centre.

Remove the packet of giblets from the body cavity of the chicken and set aside for another use.  Remove and discard the fat just inside the body and neck cavities.  Rinse the chicken, inside and out, under cold running water and then drain and blot dry, inside and out, with paper towels.  Sprinkle 2 teaspoons of the rub inside the body and neck cavities of the chicken.  Rub the bird all over on the outside with 2 teaspoons of the rub.  If you have the patience, you can put some of the rub under the skin.

Spoon the remaining rub through the holes into the beer in the can. Don't worry if it foams up.  This is normal.  Insert the beer can into the body cavity of the chicken and spread out the legs to form a sort of tripod.  tuck the wing tips behind the chicken's back.

Stand the chicken up in the centre of the hot grate, over the drip pan and away from the heat.  Cover the grill and cook the chicken until the skin is a dark golden brown and very crisp and the meat is cooked through (about 180 degrees Fahrenheit on an instant-read meat thermometer inserted in the thigh).  1-1/4 to 1-1/4 hours should do it.  If using a charcoal grill, you'll need to add 12 fresh coals per side after 1 hour.

Using tongs, carefully transfer the chicken in its upright position on the beer can to a platter and present it to your guests.  Let rest 5 minutes, then carefully remove the chicken from the beer can.  Take care not to spill the hot beer or otherwise burn yourself.
