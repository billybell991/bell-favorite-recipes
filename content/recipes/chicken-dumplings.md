- 1  5-1/2 to 6 lb boiling fowl
- 8 cups water
- 5-6 medium-large carrots, chunked
- 12 whole medium onions
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/8 tsp pepper

In large soup kettle, simmer the whole fowl with the water, covered, until tender – about 4 hours.  Be sure it is beginning to fall apart.  Left bird to utility tray and cut it into serving pieces discarding what skin and bones you readily can.  Set this meat aside for the time being.  Skim as much surplus fat as you can from the top of broth.NOTE;I usually do the above a day early so refrigerated fat is easy to scoop off the next day.  Add the carrots, onions, salt and pepper and cook until vegetables are tender.  Put chicken pieces in stew and when stew is boiling hard, put in dumplings.

DUMPLINGSI use Bisquick and follow the recipe and directions on the box.  Also, dumpling recipe is included in this book under Miscellaneous.
