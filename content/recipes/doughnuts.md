## Ingredients

- 1 cup sugar
- 3 eggs
- ½ cup less 1 tbsp soft butter
- 6 tsp (2 Tbsp) baking powder
- 4 cups sifted all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp nutmeg
- 1 tsp vanilla
- 1 cup milk

## Instructions

Into beater bowl measure the sugar, eggs and butter and beat until blended and smooth. Stir and measure the flour, add the baking powder, salt and nutmeg to it and sift again Add to first mixture along with the vanilla and milk and beat until smooth. Scrape down sides of bowl. Cover snugly and refrigerate four hours or overnight. Cut dough into quarters, scoop out ¼ with rubber scraper and out on generously floured board to ¼” thickness. (Keep remainder of dough in refrigerator.

Cut out with floured doughnut cutter, transferring doughnuts and cut-outs to board on which you can carry them to the stove. Incidentally, this dough is highly satisfactory to handle. Heat fat or oil to 390 degrees F (or hot enough to brown cube of bread in 40 seconds). Fat should be at least 1” deep or deeper if you are using a deep-fat fryer. Lift doughnuts on wide spatula and carefully slip into heated fat without crowding. As soon as they rise to surface and show gold colour on bottom, turn over. Stand over them and do not overbrown. At once list from fat with slotted spoon and drain on paper toweling

Continue with all remaining dough until it is used up, piling leftover scraps up and rolling and cutting them out too. I had 48 doughnuts and 48 hole cut-outs.

P.S. You can use this recipe to make Revolving Sputniks (Drop Doughnuts) by dropping 1” balls of dough from a teaspoon into the hot fat.
