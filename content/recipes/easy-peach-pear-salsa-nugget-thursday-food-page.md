## Ingredients

- 2.5 l (10 cups) coarsely chopped peeled peaches
- 1.25 l (5 cups) chopped peeled pears
- (about) 3 kg/6-1/2 lbs)
- (about 1.2kg/2-3/4 lbs)
- 4 limes
- 60 to 90 ml (4 to 6 Tbsp) liquid honey
- 1 – 115 g pkg commercial salsa mix

## Instructions

PREPARATION: Place 6 clean 500-ml (1 cup) Mason jars on a rack in a boiling water canner; cover jars with water to heat to a simmer (82C/180F). Set screw bands aside. Heat snap lid sealing discs in hot water, not boiling (82C/180F). Keep jars & sealing discs hot until ready to use.   2.  In a large, deep stainless-steel saucepan, combine peaches and pears. Grate peel from limes and squeeze juice. Add grated peel, 125 ml (1/2 cup) lime juice, honey and salsa mix to fruit. Stirring continuously until well mixed, bring mixture to a full rolling boil. Cook and stir for 2 minutes.  3.  Ladle hot salsa into a hot jar to within 1cm (1/2”) of top of jar (headspace). Using a non-metallic utensil, remove all bubbles and adjust headspace, if required, by adding more salsa. Wipe jar rim, removing any food residue. Centre hot sealing disc on clean jar rim. Screw band down until resistance is met, then increase to finger tip tight. Return filled jar to rack in canner. Repeat for remaining salsa.  4.  When canner is filled, ensure that all jars are covered by at least 2.5 cm (1”) of water. Cover canner and bring water to a full rolling boil before starting to count processing time. At altitudes of up to 305 m (1000 ft), process (boil filled jars) for 15 minutes for 250-ml (1-cup) jars, 20 minutes for 500-ml (2-cup) jars.  5.  When processing time is complete, remove canner lid, wait for 5 minutes, then remove jars without tilting and place them upright on a protected work surface. Let cool upright, undisturbed, for 24 hours; do not retighten screw bands.  6.  After cooling, check jar seals. Sealed discs curve downward and do not move when pressed. Remove screw bands; wipe and dry bands and jars. Store screw bands separately or replace loosely on jars, as desired. Label and store jars in a cool, dark place. For best quality, use home-canned foods within one year.  Makes about 6 500-ml (2-cup) or 12  250-ml (1-cup) jars.  Source: Bernadin
