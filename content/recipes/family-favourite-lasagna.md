- 1 lb lean ground beef
- 2 onions, chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 jar spaghetti sauce
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried basil
- Pepper to taste
- 6 lasagna noodles
- 2 cups skim or 2% cottage cheese (we use ricotta)
- 2 cups shredded partly skimmed mozzarella cheese
- 1 egg, lightly beaten
- 1/2 cup freshly grated parmesan

In a large nonstick skillet or Dutch oven, cook beef over medium heat for about 5 minutes, or until browned, breaking up with a fork.  Pour off fat.  Add onions and garlic; cook for 3 to 5 minutes, or until softened.  Add spaghetti sauce, oregano and basil; bring to a boil.  Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer, uncovered, stirring occasionally for 15 minutes.  Season with salt and pepper to taste.

In a large pot of boiling water, cook lasagna noodles for 10 to 12 minutes, or until tender but firm.  Drain and rinse under cold water; drain well.  Combine cottage cheese, mozzarella cheese, egg and 1/2 of the Parmesan cheese.  Cover bottom of a 13x9 inch baking dish sparingly with some of the tomato sauce.  top with a layer of lasagna noodles.  Cover with 1/2 of the cheese mixture.  Cover with 1/2 of the remaining tomato sauce, noodles and cheese to make 2 layers of each, then a final layer of tomato sauce.  Sprinkle with remaining Parmesan cheese.  Bake, uncovered, in 350 degree Fahrenheit oven for 45 minutes, or until hot and bubbly.  Remove from oven and let stand for 5 to 10 minutes before serving.  Makes 8 servings.
