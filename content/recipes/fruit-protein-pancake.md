## Ingredients

- 3 bananas
- 2 eggs
- 3 Tbps rolled oats
- Olive oil

## Instructions

1. Whisk both eggs in a bowl.
2. Mash up just 1 of the 3 bananas on a plate, using a fork.  Add to eggs and mix.
3. Add the rolled oats to the egg/banana mix as well.
4. Cut up the other 2 bananas in slices.
5. In a pan (that you can cover), drizzle some olive oil to make sure the bottom is barely covered.
6. Lay down all of the banana slices evenly around the pan, then pour the egg mixture over them.  Even it out with a spoon or your spatula.
7. Cover and cook on low for 10 mins.
8. Flip it over and cook on low for another 5 mins (still covered).  Pro-tip: Saw a neat trick to hold a plate upside down over your pan, tightly, then flip the pan and plate right over - to flip your pancake.  Worked like a charm!
9. Cut it up and put some syrup or whipped cream or whatever you want to jazz it up!

Weight Watchers Points: 1 point for 1/3 of the pancake.

Billy's Notes:  This was pretty good!  Ate 1/2 of it and was full in the morning.  Great protein and super healthy.  Used sugar free syrup on it but I'm not even sure it needed syrup.  Also, we only had one banana so I used that in the batter and just used strawberries and frozen blueberries instead of the other 2 bananas. It came out as kind of a french toast meets banana bread type of thing.
