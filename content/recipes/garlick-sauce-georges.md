## Ingredients

- 1/4 cup Garlic cloves, peeled (approx. 10–12 cloves)
- 1/2 tsp Kosher salt (plus more to taste)
- 1 Large Egg white (at room temperature)
- 1 cup Vegetable oil (or other neutral oil like Canola or Grapeseed)
- 1.5 tbsp Fresh lemon juice

## Instructions

1. Prep the Garlic: Slice the garlic cloves in half and remove any green sprouts (the germ) to prevent bitterness.
2. Create the Paste: Place the garlic and salt in a food processor. Pulse until finely minced. Scrape down the sides and process on High until it forms a smooth, thick paste.
3. Note: If the blades aren't catching the garlic, add 1 tsp of oil to help it move.
4. Incorporate the Egg: Add the egg white to the processor. Run on High for about 30 seconds until the mixture is bright white and frothy.
5. The Slow Emulsion (The Secret): With the processor running on High, begin adding the vegetable oil in an extremely slow, steady stream (think droplets or a thin thread). Take your time; this should take about 2–3 minutes for the full cup.
6. Balance with Lemon: Once you have added about half of the oil and the sauce is thickening, drizzle in half of the lemon juice. Continue with the remaining oil, and finish with the rest of the lemon juice.
7. Final Whip: Once all ingredients are in, process for another 15–20 seconds until the sauce is stiff, airy, and holds its shape on a spoon.
