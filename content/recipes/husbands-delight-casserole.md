1 lb lean ground beef (I (Mom) used 1-1/2 lb gr. Moose hamburg

2 cloves garlic, finely chopped

1 tsp sugar

1 tsp salt ( I (Mom) omitted salt and it tasted just fine)

¼ tsp pepper

2 8-oz tins tomato sauce

4 cups dry egg noodles (1/2 lb)

5 scallions, chopped (I (Mom) used regular onions)

1 pkg (3 oz) cream cheese, room temp.

1 8-oz container sour cream

½ cup or more shredded cheddar cheese

1. In a large skillet, cook meat over medium high heat, breaking up clumps with wooden

spoon, until browned, 5 minutes. Carefully drain off drippings. Stir in garlic, sugar, salt,

pepper and tomato sauce into meat. Cover, simmer 15 minutes.

2. Meanwhile, in large, deep pot of lightly salted water, cook noodles just until al dente,

firm yet tender, following package directions. Drain.

3. In small bowl, stir together scallions, cream cheese and sour cream until well blended.

4. Lightly coat bottom of shallow 2-1/2 quart baking dish (I used a 9 x 13” Pyrex ) with

non-stick cooking spray. Arrange noodles over bottom of dish. Spoon meat sauce

evenly over noodles. Carefully spread sour cream mixture over top. Sprinkle with

cheddar cheese. (Can be made up to 24 hours ahead and refrigerated, covered.)

5. Heat oven to 350 degrees. Bake casserole until heated through and bubbly, about 30

minutes. Let stand 10 minutes before serving.

NOTE: I (Mom)  would put a little butter in the noodles.
