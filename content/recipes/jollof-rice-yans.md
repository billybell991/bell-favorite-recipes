## Ingredients

- 2 bell peppers (ideally red, but I had 1 green and 1 red)
- 3 roma tomatoes
- 2 red onions (barf)
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers
- 3 garlic cloves
- 1 tsp fresh ginger
- Half a cup water
- ⅔ cup vegetable oil
- 1 can tomato paste
- 1 tbsp curry powder
- 2 tsp dried thyme
- 3 chicken bouillon cubes
- Bay leaves
- 3 cups parboiled long grain rice
- Salt and pepper to taste (be generous)

## Instructions

1. Roughly chop up bell peppers, tomatoes, 1 of the onions, scotch bonnet peppers, garlic and ginger. Throw that into a blender, add half a cup of water, and get it all liquidy.

1. Get your rage out and chop up the remaining onion into lil bits. If you cry, it’s just the lil bitch leaving your body in preparation for the scotch bonnet realness. Add the vegetable oil to the pot and let that warm up on medium heat, then add the onion you just diced. Cook that for 3 minutes.

1. Add the tomato paste and stir that in and let that cook for 5 minutes.

1. Add the blender contents into the pot and stir that up. Bring it to a simmer and then partially cover cuz this shit will splatter. Let that bubble for 15 minutes, stirring it now and then.

1. While waiting for that, it's a good time to rinse the rice.  I rinsed it a bunch, and then let it drain over the sink.

1. Throw in the thyme, curry powder, bouillon cubes, bay leaves, and salt and pepper to taste. I didn’t measure the salt and pepper but it says “season generously”. Bring to a boil on medium-high heat.

1. Once the concoction is boiling, add the rinsed rice to it, bring it to a boil, immediately drop heat to medium-low.  Cover the top of the pot with a sheet of aluminium foli and then cover it with the pot lid.  Let it cook for 30 minutes. You don't have to stir it.  Just let it do its thing.
2. After that 30 mins is up, take it off the heat and leave the foil and lid on.  Let it steam for around 15 mins.
3. Give it a quick fluff and you're good to go.
