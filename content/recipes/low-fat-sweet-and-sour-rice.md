## Ingredients

Note:  Make the sauce below for 1 cup of pre-cooked rice.  Just double or triple it for however many cups of rice you have.

Make the rice however you make your rice.  This isn't a rice recipe - it's more of a sauce recipe.

- 2 Tbsp low sodium soya sauce
- 2 Tbsp rice vinegar
- 1.5 Tbsp pineapple juice  (NOTE: don't be a dope like me and buy pineapple juice.  Just use the juice from the can of pineapple, noted below)
- 1 Tbsp tomato paste
- 1 Tbsp brown sugar
- 1 Tsp corn starch
- 1 Tbsp water (for corn starch slurry)
- 1 Medium carrot, julienned
- 1 Medium red bell pepper, cut thinly
- 1 Medium green bell pepper, cut thinly
- 1/2 cup of pineapple chunks (optional, but really, there's pineapple juice in it for cryin' out loud.  If you don't like pineapple, don't make this)

## Instructions

1. Cook your rice ahead of time however you cook rice.
2. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, pineapple juice, tomato paste, and brown sugar to create the sweet and sour sauce. Stir until well combined.
3. In a separate small bowl, dissolve the cornstarch in 1 tablespoon of water to create a slurry. Set aside.
4. Heat a large nonstick skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the julienned carrots and sliced bell peppers. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until they are slightly softened but still have some crunch.
5. Pour the sweet and sour sauce into the skillet with the vegetables. Stir well to coat the vegetables evenly.
6. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Gradually add the cornstarch slurry while stirring to thicken the sauce. Allow it to cook for 2-3 minutes until the sauce reaches the desired consistency.
7. If using, gently fold in the pineapple chunks and cook for an additional minute to warm them through.
8. Fluff the cooked rice with a fork and serve it onto plates or bowls. Spoon the sweet and sour vegetable mixture over the rice.
