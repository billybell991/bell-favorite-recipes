I've made this one so many times, I can recite it from memory.  A favorite of Pep's (and pretty much anyone else).  So good that it convinced my girls that tomatoes aren't the devil in stewed vegetable form.

## Ingredients

- 5 handfuls of elbow macaroni  (Yep, handfuls.  Not sure how, but my hand-size is the same as Mom's and everyone else...but it still somehow works!)
- 1 brick of medium cheddar cheese (I think they're 400g bricks, but those long 2x4 sized ones)
- Margarine
- Salt/Pepper to taste
- 1 Can of Stewed Whole Tomatoes
- Water

## Instructions

In a pot, boil enough water to boil 5 handfuls of macaroni.  I throw a bit of salt and olive oil in there (for taste and so the noodles won't stick).

Grate the entire brick of cheese while you're waiting for the noodles to cook.

Boil the noodles till they're "el dente" (whatever that means...I think it's Italian for "however you like them").

Drain the noodles and put them back in the pot.

Melt enough margarine in with the noodles so they're good and greasy.  Usually around a tablespoon or so.

Dump the can of whole tomatoes in there, but chop them up.  I use a potato masher to make them as small as possible so the girls don't pick them out, but Mom used to chop them by hand with a paring knife.

Put a handful of the grated cheese in with the noodle mixture and mix it in (the noodles will be cooler now, so it won't melt - it's for extra yummy gooey yum-ness when it cooks later on).

Sprinkle some salt and pepper in there to taste (can always be added later when it's in your bowl, so your call).

Dump the noodle mixture into a 9x13 casserole and cover it with the rest of the shredded cheese.  Make sure to cover all the noodles or they'll be exposed and get hard when you bake them.

Bake in a 300 degree Fahrenheit oven for 30 mins.
