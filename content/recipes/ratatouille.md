## Ingredients

- 1 small eggplant about 3 cups, cubed into 1/4-inch pieces
- 2 tsp kosher salt
- 4 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oildivided
- 1 medium onion about 1 1/2 cups, diced
- 2 zucchini about 3 cups, cubed into 1/4-inch pieces
- 6 cloves garlic about 2 Tbsp, minced
- 2 bell peppers about 3 cups, cubed into 1/2-inch pieces
- 5 whole basil leaves plus 1 Tbsp thinly sliced basil leaves
- 1/2 tsp crushed red pepperor to taste
- 1/2 tsp freshly groundblack pepperor to taste
- 1 Tbsp capers
- 2 1/2 cups tomatoes chopped into 1-inch pieces
- 1 Tbsp parsley chopped
- 1 tsp good qualitybalsamic vinegar
- Additional olive oil and balsamic vinegar for drizzling

## Instructions

1. Sprinkle salt on the eggplant, toss to coat and allow to drain in a colander set over a bowl.
2. In 'Saute' mode, heat 2 Tbsp olive oil.
3. Add onion, and cook until translucent, about 3 to 5 minutes. Stir occasionally.
4. Add zucchini and garlic, saute 3 to 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
5. Add bell pepper, saute 3 to 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
6. Add whole basil leaves and stir.
7. Press 'Cancel' and transfer contents of inner pot into a large bowl and set aside.
8. In 'Saute' mode, heat 2 Tbsp olive oil.
9. Add drained eggplant and saute until lightly browned, stirring frequently.Be sure to prevent it from burning.
10. Add back in reserved vegetables, crushed red pepper, black pepper, and capers.
11. Stir gently, making sure that there are no brown bits sticking to the bottom of the inner pot. *
12. Top with tomatoes.
13. Cook on 'Manual' (or 'Pressure Cook') mode for 10 minutes.  Make sure pressure release handle is in 'Sealing' position.
14. Do a quick release of pressure.
15. Press ‘Cancel’ and open the Instant Pot.
16. The ratatouille will have some liquid.
17. In 'Saute' mode, simmer until the Instant Pot ratatouille has thickened up, about 6 to 8 minutes.
18. Sprinkle with chopped basil and parsley, drizzle additional olive oil and balsamic vinegar over the top.
19. Serve as a side dish, with pasta,  with a medley of salads, or on a toasted baguette topped with goat cheese.
