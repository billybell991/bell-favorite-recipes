## Ingredients

- 1 elephant roast
- ½ boxcar of bread crumbs
- 3 crates of cut celery
- 3 mailbags of chopped garlic cloves
- 2 trash cans of scalding hot water
- 50 lbs diced hot peppers
- 1 keg salt
- 2 oil drums of butter

## Instructions

Blast off the rear end of one elephant to make large opening for stuffing. Mix ½ boxcar of bread crumbs, 3 mailbags of chopped garlic, 3 crates of cut celery and 2 trash cans of scalding hot water to moisten.

Add 50 lbs of diced hot peppers and 10 shovelfuls of salt. Place in an empty cement mixer and stir gently with a railroad

tie.  Skewer elephant with one redwood tree (hickory can be used if you are a conservationist). Close rear end with telephone cable.

Place each end of redwood (hickory) on a 40 foot crane for support. Cook over bonfire for 3 months. (An additional month is needed for every ton over 10,000 lbs).

Remove from the fire and place in an airplane hangar for 2 weeks to cool. Baste with butter over the meat with a strong warehouse broom.

Slice with a chain saw and serve.
