## Ingredients

- 4 red peppers
- Half a red onion
- 4 cloves of garlic
- 2 medium sized potatoes (yellow or white)
- A carton of vegetable broth
- Salt & Pepper (to taste)
- Cayenne pepper (to taste)
- A gob of butter

Instructions:

## Instructions

1. Turn on your oven broiler to high.
2. Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil and spray with some cooking spray.
3. Cut each pepper into fours and line on the baking sheet.
4. Put in oven and let broil until skins start to turn black.
5. Once skins have turned black, take out of oven and put into a large mixing bowl.  Place plate on top of it to trap the heat in.  This helps the skin soften so they are easy to remove.  Let the peppers stay in the bowl for 10 minutes.
6. While you wait, mince your onion and garlic.
7. Wash and peel potatoes and cut them into cubes.
8. Once 10 minutes have passed, carefully peel the skins off the peppers.
9. Once the skin has been peeled off, chop up the peppers.
10. In a large soup pot, melt butter at the bottom over medium heat.  Combine your onion and garlic.  Sauté for about 2 minutes, until the onions become soft.
11. Add potatoes and combine for about another minute.
12. Add in red peppers and stir everything together for about a minute.
13. Season with salt and pepper.
14. Empty carton of vegetable broth into the pot, stirring occasionally.
15. Let soup simmer on low to medium heat, until potatoes are soft.
16. Carefully sample the broth and add cayenne pepper if needed for an extra bit of spice.
17. Carefully remove from stovetop and use immersion blender to bend sopu to a velvety texture.
