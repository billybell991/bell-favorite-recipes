## Ingredients

- 2 28-0z cans best quality tomatoes (Drained   2 6-oz cans tomato paste
- 2 green peppers
- 4 medium cooking onions
- 6 lg outer celery stalks w/leaves
- ¾ cup sugar
- 2 tsp salt
- ¾ cup vinegar
- Spices tied in spice bag

## Instructions

Spices tied in cheesecloth bag with long string: 12 whole cloves, 1 tsp mustard seed, 1 tsp celery seed, 3” cinnamon stick, ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (OR 2 Tbsp mixed pickling spices). Into rather heavy kettle measure the tomatoes from which excess juice has been drained to shorten boiling period. (Use juice for some other purpose.) Add the peppers, onions and celery that have been put through medium coarse knife of grinder. You will have to reverse the winding handle several times to unpack the celery strands that gather behind the knife. OR chop finely by hand on board. Add vinegar and salt and sugar and spices tied in bag. Bring to boil and stir very frequently to prevent sticking. Boil gently ½ hour. Add tomato paste and boil ½ hour longer. Remove spice bag. Pour into 3 sterilized pint jars and seal.

NOTE: The use of whole spices in a bag and addition of tomato paste give this chili sauce the good color and texture common to commercial chili sauce.
