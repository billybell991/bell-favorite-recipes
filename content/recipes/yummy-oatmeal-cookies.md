## Ingredients

- 1 cup butter or margarine
- 1-1/2 cups brown sugar
- 2 eggs
- ½ cup milk
- ½ tsp white vinegar (I (Mom) didn’t use this)
- 1-3/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp baking powder
- ¼ tsp salt
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- 3 cups quick-cooking rolled oats
- ½ cup raisins (optional)
- ½ cup chopped walnuts or sliced almonds (optional)

## Instructions

Cream together butter and brown sugar. Add the vinegar to the milk and set it aside for one minute. Add eggs to the butter and sugar mixture one at a time, mixing until light and fluffy. Add the milk with vinegar and mix until well incorporated.

In a separate bowl, sift together flour, soda, baking powder, salt and cinnamon. Stir into the creamed mixture. Stir in rolled oats and raisins and nuts if desired.

Drop from tablespoon, two inches apart on a greased cookie sheet. Top with a walnut half if desired. Bake in a 350 degree (180 C) oven for approximately eight to 10 minutes (I (Mom) used 12 minutes) – more if you like your cookies crispy, less if you like your cookies soft and chewy.

Cool slightly and remove from cookie sheet. Makes about 60 cookies.
