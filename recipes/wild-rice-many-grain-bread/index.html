Wild Rice Many-Grain Bread

Freshly baked golden-brown bread with ½ cup wild rice and granulated sugar that fills the kitchen with the most incredible aroma. The kind of recipe that makes you wonder why you ever bought store-bought.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup wild rice
  • 2 Tbsp granulated sugar
  • ½ cup warm water
  • 1 pkg dry yeast (or 1 Tbsp )
  • 5 cups (approx.) all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup fancy molasses
  • 2 Tbsp shortening
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ cup natural bran
  • ½ cup raw sunflower seeds
  • ¾ cup 7-grain cereal
  • ¾ cup rolled oats

Instructions

  1.  In saucepan, cook wild rice in 2 cups water (500 ml) water for about 45 minutes or until tender; drain and let cool.

  2.  Meanwhile, dissolve 1 tsp (5 ml) of the sugar in warm water. Sprinkle in yeast; let soften, stirring together until dissolved. Stir in ¼ cup (50 ml) of the flour to make a thin paste; set in warm place for 10 minutes or until bubbly.

  3.  In large bowl and using electric mixer, combine molasses, remaining sugar, shortening, salt and 2 cups (500 ml) warm water. In order, beat in bran, sunflower seeds, yeast mixture, wild rice, 7-grain cereal and rolled oats. With wooden spoon, beat in as much of the remaining flour as necessary to make firm dough. Turn out onto lightly floured surface; knead for about 10 minutes or until smooth and elastic, kneading in as much of the remaining flour as necessary..

  4.  Place in large greased bowl, turning to grease all over. Cover with plastic wrap; let rice in warm draft-free place until doubled in bulk, about 2 hours. (NOTE: I tried the plastic wrap and found the dough stuck to it even if it was lightly greased; I choose instead to make sure that there is flour on the surface and then cover it with a clean cloth.)

  5.  Punch down dough; turn out onto lightly floured surface. Divide into thirds. With rolling pin, flatten each to about 7-1/2-inch (19 cm) square; firmly roll up each into log. Place each in greased 8- x  4-inch pans. Cover and let rise until doubled in bulk, about 1-1/2 hours.

  6.  Bake in center of 375 F (190 C) oven for 15 minutes or until loaves sound hollow when tapped on bottom. Remove from pans; let cool on racks. Makes 3 loaves, 12 slices each.
